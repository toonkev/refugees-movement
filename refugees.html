Population movements of refugees

By Kevin Choi
June 24, 2021 — 7.55pm
, register or subscribe to save articles for later.
Advertisement

People have to go somewhere when Syrians are driven out of their homes. And the majority ended up in concentration camps: IDP camps in Syria, or refugee camps in neighbouring countries such as Lebanon, Turkey and Jordan. These countries are extended to their limits via the flow of people both from the host countries and naturally from the refugees themselves.

Approximately half the whole population of Syria was rooted in the struggles. There's no home to return to for many of them. In 2013 the population of Lebanon was about four and five million; approximately one and a quarter of Syrian refugees had grown. Approximately one tenth of Jordan's population in 2013 has been taken over. More than 200,000 Syrians have even been taken by Iraqi Kurdistan, now in a battle against ISIS. The local administration has very difficulty dealing with these massive influxes of populations. The settlements absorb many resources which may not be very large and politically disruptive in state municipalities.

CARE is a humanitarian organisation dedicated to worldwide poverty reduction. They seek to assist transformation through enhancement of auto-help ability, the provision of economical opportunities, emergency relief, policy influence and discriminatory response. In Jordan, a bordering nation of Syria where many refugees fled, CARE provides humanitarian programmes. CARE has two big refugee camps. According to the United Nations Refugee Agency, there are roughly 11,000 refugees in Azraw and around 83,000 in the Zaatari camp. In Jordan, Lebanon, Egypt, Yemen and Syria, the organisation and its partners worked on the ground to assist displaced persons and communities in the war. Recently, Serbia and Croatia have extended their aid to support Syrian refugees who are escaping to Europe.

Doctors Without Borders, a medical and humanitarian aid organization founded in 1991, is funded primarily through private donations. It aims to offer professional help to those in need in a variety of circumstances such as natural disaster, armed conflicts, food crises, epidemics, or population displacement. The organization has been significantly involved in the widespread population displacement in Syria. The organization’s work inside the country has been severely limited due to conflict. In 2014, staff within Syria were abducted and subsequently released, leading to the organization cutting support to areas where the Islamic State is in control. This has left millions of people in areas that have no access to external aid or assistance. Doctors Without Borders runs medical facilities within Syria and supports over 150 medical structures in the region, despite their extensive obstacles and hazards. It also supports the Syrian refugees who are fled to Jordan, Lebanon and Iraq. In 2015 it began deploying Mediterranean Search and Rescue Ships with the goal of rescuing Syrians trying to sail to Europe by boat.

Refugees International is committed to promoting international protection and aid to displaced persons and promoting solutions to the displacement crisis. Refugees International does not take money from government agencies or the UN and does not have personnel on the ground that provide direct assistance. The group is instead looking at the situations faced by refugees to provide suggestions to other international assistance organisations.
